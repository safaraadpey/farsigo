# Frequently Asked Questions

# What is FarsiGo?

FarsiGo is an AI-assisted simplified fork of Persian designed for learning, writing, and communication.

---

# Is FarsiGo a new language?

No.

FarsiGo is a simplified form of Persian.

It preserves Persian vocabulary, culture, and identity while simplifying grammar.

---

# Does FarsiGo replace Persian?

No.

Persian remains the original language.

FarsiGo is an alternative simplified variant for everyday communication and learning.

---

# Why simplify Persian?

Many grammatical rules repeat information that is already clear from the context.

FarsiGo removes unnecessary complexity while preserving meaning.

Example:

Standard Persian

دیروز من به مدرسه رفتم.

FarsiGo

دیروز من مدرسه رفتن.

The word "دیروز" already expresses the past.

---

# Is Pinglish supported?

Yes.

FarsiGo officially supports both Persian script and Pinglish.

Example:

من می غذا خوردن.

man mi ghaza khordan.

---

# Who created FarsiGo?

FarsiGo is a community project initiated with the assistance of AI.

The language is expected to evolve through discussion, experimentation, and community contributions.

---

# Is FarsiGo open source?

Yes.

Anyone can read the documentation, suggest improvements, discuss grammar, and contribute to the project.

---

# Why is it called FarsiGo?

The name reflects a Persian that is easier to learn, easier to write, and easier to use.

"Go" represents movement, simplicity, and accessibility.

---

# Can I contribute?

Yes.

Ideas, grammar improvements, examples, translations, and discussions are all welcome.
